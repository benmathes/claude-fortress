# Libashedan — Strategic Direction

*Updated each session. This is the game plan, not the history (see chronicle.md).*

---

## Fortress Philosophy

**Trading Hub.** The mountain is the asset. Stone crafts sustain us until we can export more valuable goods. Survive winters, court the autumn caravan, grow cautiously.

---

## Current Priorities (Mid Spring → Autumn, Year 1)

1. **Fix the mining blockage** — Cancel all suspended `>>` designations. Place a fresh `d→d` plain mine on adjacent `▓` rock directly accessible from the surface. Verify the job appears in `j` list before unpausing.

2. **Dig a basic underground entrance** — A horizontal corridor into the mountain, 3–5 tiles deep, enough to build workshops underground before winter.

3. **Build a Craftsdwarf's Workshop** (`b → w → r`) — Stukos (Stonecrafter) and Athel (Stoneworker) need a place to work. Stone crafts = trade goods.

4. **Build a Trade Depot** (`b → d`) — Must be on accessible ground before the autumn caravan arrives. Without a depot, no trading.

5. **Stockpile finished goods** — Designate a stockpile room near the depot for stone crafts.

---

## Strategic Tradeoffs

- **Survival even over wealth** — Do not chase elaborate rooms or deep shafts. Get shelter and warmth before winter.
- **Trade even over military** — No military investment until the fortress can sustain itself economically. Walls and locks before soldiers.
- **Stone crafts even over exotic goods** — We have stone in abundance. Use what we have.

---

## Known Threats / Watchlist

- **Autumn caravan approaching** — No depot, no trade. This is the immediate deadline.
- **Stukos is a necromancer** — Keep an eye on him. Dwarven fortresses and necromancers have a complicated history.
- **No shelter yet** — 10 dwarves sleeping outside. Winter will be harsh in The Eviscerated Spikes.
- **Migrants will keep coming** — Mouths to feed without food production yet. Fish Cleaner Dumat needs water access.
