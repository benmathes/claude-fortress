# The Chronicle of Libashedan — "Axestirred"

*As recorded by the Expedition Scribe, Year 250*

---

## Spring → Early Summer, 250

Seven dwarves struck the earth at Libashedan in the year 250. Their leader, Bim Kosothtâmol, planted the expedition's wagon on a hillside where rock lay close beneath the soil. Among the seven: Dastot Vúshurist, the Miner — the one upon whom all underground hope rested.

The early days were not without incident. Designations were placed for a staircase descent — the first step toward a Trading Hub and warm rooms before winter. But the mountain did not yield. Dastot stood idle, pickaxe in hand, staring at the red-marked rock. All seven dwarves milled about, purposeless. The game clock ticked. The sun moved. Nobody dug.

Investigations consumed days. Were they trapped in a burrow? No — none had been defined. Was the game merely paused, their stillness an illusion? Sometimes yes, sometimes no. Were the down-staircase designations pointing somewhere unreachable? That remained the open wound.

Summer arrived unearned. The caravan would come in autumn. The trading hub existed only as ambition.

Then — two waves of migrants stumbled into Libashedan. First one Bowyer named Tulon, then a Metalcrafter named Olin, then a Stonecrafter named Stukos (who was, unsettlingly, a necromancer). The population swelled to ten. A cat adopted Dumat the Fish Cleaner. Dumat and the Jeweler Thîkut married. The world kept spinning even as the mine sat silent.

At session's end: ten dwarves, no shelter dug, no workshop built, autumn caravan approaching. The mystery of the idle miner remains unsolved.
